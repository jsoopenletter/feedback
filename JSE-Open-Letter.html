To Just Stop Oil,

We are a collective of individuals made up of BIPOC (Black, Indigenous and People of Colour), Queer, Neurodiverse, Working Class and Young people. Some of us have been involved in your campaign at various levels and some of us could have been involved if not for our experiences and genuine fears surrounding it.

As environmentalists, we are not against the goals of JSO, however there are a number of issues that urgently need addressing within your campaign. This is not infighting, this is feedback. It is not personal and these issues certainly aren't exclusive to your campaign, but they are symptomatic of wider issues across the climate movement. We believe this to be amplified by your emphasis on urgency, lack of community care and divisive messaging.

We came together as a group to manage the wellbeing of marginalised people affected by the issues with the JSO campaign. As individuals, this is not the first time we have tried to engage with JSO. Many of us have tried reaching out and have been ignored, gaslit, dismissed and guilt tripped.

Some of our members have experienced harrasment by oppressive behaviours from JSO members, and we have therefore chosen to stay anonymous. Most of us who have been in the climate movement for a while can see how it is changing. We deserve to feel as safe as everyone else in our own movement, a movement that we helped shape and build.

This letter highlights various issues including: Urgency culture, wellbeing, and impact on other groups. We are asking you to believe marginalised people and act accordingly. Statements from indivduals across the climate movement are included throughout this letter. Moving forward, we will provide guidance for your responsibilities at this time.


CULTURE OF URGENCY

The Just Stop Oil campaign is being driven by urgency culture, a narrative in which reactionary and immediate action is framed as the only path to solving the climate crisis. This alarmist messaging gives way to the harmful excuse that the climate movement simply cannot 'waste time' on inclusion, respect, and accountability. It implies that we don't have enough time to build relationships, help each other and create communities of care.

"I’ve been part of Extinction Rebellion since 2019 and have seen how it has changed since the emergence of Just Stop Oil. In many ways, it feels like XR has been co-opted by JSO to fulfil their own goals at the expense of the wider movement using a ‘sense of urgency’ narrative and dismissing any concerns raised as ‘infighting’ rather than respecting the movement and the role it plays within environmentalism."

Concerned Climate Activist

This atmosphere of urgency is also a serious wellbeing concern. Action upon action, alert upon alert, generating an environment in which people are held in states of tension and stress for extended periods of time. When we are in these states of stress response we are far more likely to put ourselves in positions of physical and mental risk, especially when encouraged to do so by those around us, who may be in similar states of distress. 

“My other main worry is the way that young people have been put into situations where anyone would struggle to make informed decisions. I don't want to be patronising to young people, but I think the JSO model showed a lack of care in taking very young, very new activists and putting them in a situation of extended sleep deprivation and heightened emotions.

The narrative of sacrifice in this context has pushed young people in the movement to make decisions I don't think they otherwise would make and that potentially will have caused long-term damage. I believe that care for life is one of the most important tools we have in fighting against ecofascism and I don't think this is displayed when we emphasise sacrifice over everything else.”

Climate Justice Activist

Urgency culture, paired with themes of sacrifice, honour, responsibility and matryrdom, is leading people to repeatedly put themselves in harms way. This is already reaching dangerous levels, and has the potential to result in self injurous behaviour, suicide and interpersonal violence. 

When our decision making abilities are severely impacted by states of stress response, we are much less immune to the themes above (sacrifice etc.), especially when pushed by urgency culture and those around us. This allows many of the issues raised within this letter to continue to grow and multiply unchallenged.

One such issue which connects sacrifice, matryrdom, and people putting themselves in positions of harm is the glorification of arrest and imprisonment.

GLORIFICATION OF ARREST AND PRISON

"The first time I was arrested, I had to go through months of therapy for PTSD. Just Stop Oil's glorification of arrest and imprisonment, coupled with their disregard for the ways in which marginalised people experience policing and the criminal justice system, is encouraging young people to repeatedly put themselves in positions of physical and mental harm.

Framing this arrest-based strategy as some sort of greatest sacrifice further ostracises marginalised people, and is a slap in the face to those who have committed so much to this movement through a beautifully diverse range of tactics.

My transness has undeniably affected my experience of arrest because the criminal justice system is inherently linked to oppressive systems, and therefore cannot be weaponised against those same systems which are driving climate injustice."

Trans Climate Activist

Using someone's testimonial of more positive experience of prison time to persuade others is not informed consent. A person of colour or trans person for example, would experience arrest and the criminal justice system very differently, however this is not addressed within JSO training.

In contrast, arrest and imprisonment are encouraged to the point at which numbered targets were found to have been set for the latter. This fixation on numbers and using prison and remand quotas to mobilise people into climate action is misleading. You cannot quantify the moment that sparks a movement to achieve success. To insinuate that you have all the answers, leading people on this irreversible path, is irresponsible. Prisons are designed to dissapear people away from sight, so that they can be forgotten. 

Lack of informed consent around arrest and imprisonment could lead to extreme structural violence towards people from marginalised groups taking part in the campaign. Some examples of legislation being used against them could include the PCSC Bill and The Nationality and Borders Bill. Solidarity isn't embedded or considered, when included it's often just to tick a box. 

"A particular incident that still makes me sick to the stomach when I think about it is when multiple FINT (female, intersex, non-binary and trans) people of colour delivered talks at a festival regarding their experiences of state violence, surveillance, the social care system, the legal system, and the prison system. JSO people attended these talks and heard of the harrowing and traumatic experiences marginalised people face when we not only take action but simply just exist.

One of their activists then had the audacity to stand up and deliver a talk on how great they believed prison was and in there words how much fun it was for them. They paraded around laughing and joking with the clothes they were made to wear in prison (prison greys), the same style clothes that people of colour are abused in, are beaten in and many even die in whilst in the hands of the so-called criminal justice system. The criminal justice system is no joke or laughing matter. Those who choose to put themselves there will never understand what it is like to exist everyday with the fear that the state could kidnap you and imprison you for simply just existing with a different tone of skin.

After the talk I spoke to multiple people, most of them in fact white who told me of their disgust at the insensitivity that the JSO activists had and of their concerns of the wider culture surrounding JSO. I would like to mention I have been unlawfully arrested, surveilled, harassed and relentlessly stop and searched, beaten, and bruised all at the hands of the police. I suffer the psychological consequences of this every day. The trauma that I have faced has meant that at times I have been so anxious I could not go outside, so scared that I’ve ran and hid and sometimes even screamed in the street when I’ve seen the police because I’ve felt so afraid of what they could do to me.

My quality of life has drastically declined because of this anxiety, nightmares, flashbacks, and inability to sleep. I’m also someone who has given much time to the climate movement, including the last two years full time without any financial support.

For JSO people to then tell me I’m not doing enough, makes me want to scream into a void like the mad woman that state violence has made me. When movements like JSO glorify arrest and prison many people open the door of a lifetime of fear and harassment without consent and knowing the risks. I’m not asking JSO to change because I don’t believe they can. What I’m trying to do is appeal to the people in JSO who are feeling concerned, unsafe or disenfranchised to leave and find a more just and supportive way of working under a different banner.

Anti-fascist means standing against all forms of fascism and that includes eco-fascism."

Brown, Queer, Working Class Lass​​​​​​​​​​​​​​

APPEALING TO FAR RIGHT IDEOLOGIES

"When I saw the Nigel Farage post, I was really on edge for a couple of days from the anxiety that it caused. The people in Just Stop Oil are my friends, my comrades and people I see every day... I was shocked that they would want to be associated with someone who doesn't want people like me to exist. Of all the people to use.. why was he chosen for this post?

As people of colour we know what he represents, he is known for his racism and for vilifying refugees. I am a child of a refugee myself, so this made me feel unsafe in the climate movement.

When we brought it up with people in JSO, we weren't listened and told that it was 'clearly a joke'. It was project that they were working on for a long time and I understand that criticism was hard to hear but I was disspointed that they were unable to look past their own experiences to listen to me. This did not feel like a joke. They don't have to face public figures who would laugh in jubilation if I died."

BIPOC Activist from the Movement of Movements

Activists from marginalised groups deserve to feel safe in their own movements. For you, appealing to right wing thinking may be a shortcut to widening public support. For us, this means violence towards our communities through structural and interpersonal racism, classism and ableism.

LACK OF RESPECT FOR OTHER GROUPS

The idea that 'our way is the only way' and 'if you are not with us you are against us' is rooted in colonialist binary thinking. We are seeing a pattern of infiltration, coersion and co-option all across the country. Your supporters have fragmented and broken apart many local groups. There is no respect for all the important roles different groups play in the wider climate movement through diversity of tactics.

"Due to the legitimate issues raised about Just Stop Oil, I have voiced concerns to Extinction Rebellion in several spaces about forming a coalition and platforming their campaign until these issues have been addressed.

Unfortunately, due to the number of XR roles carried out by rebels who are also involved or closely affiliated with JSO it’s made it increasingly difficult to continue voicing my concerns as I’ve experienced gaslighting, and have been met with dismissiveness or been ignored.

Over time, this has had a detrimental impact on my psychological wellbeing to the point where I no longer feel comfortable in these spaces and have had to step down from my role in XR and leave the movement."

Climate Justice Activist 

"The relationship with JSO appears to have blurred the lines when it comes to implementing XR UK’s strategy and the immediate demand to end the fossil fuel economy. This has caused much tension and divide in my region.

It was particularly apparent at the last Rebellion when several local groups and some rebels made the decision not to attend due to concerns about the relationship with JSO not being addressed. To say I was surprised to see JSO at the Rebellion handing out leaflets and trying to recruit rebels, some of whom I knew to be marginalised people who had already been affected and/or expressed concerns about the JSO campaign would be an understatement.

To then find out they had been given permission by XR to be there, despite so many concerns about JSO being raised I was left feeling like they were determined to work together at any cost. Since then, nothing much has changed – JSO haven’t addressed the issues and conversations about supporting the JSO campaign and working together still dominate many XR spaces."

Social Justice Activist

As people from the movement of movements, we take issue with you borrowing people from our communities, only to burn them out and traumatise them. When they come back into our spaces, they are not the same. You abandon them. Leaving them unwell with no aftercare and no responsibility. We have to pick up the pieces for your negligence and lack of respect for human life. They are not just pawns, they are people. 

"Before the actions I was promised that all legal fees, fines, or court costs would be covered by JSO. This was a massive assurance as I am a student and don't have an endless supply of financial support. This promise made me more willing to take action. As a result I now have 1 conviction and 3 upcoming trials.

When I started approaching people to get the financial support in place I was passed around multiple individuals, some of whom just suggested starting a Crowdfunder to cover costs. It was then suggested that I would only be eligible for costs to be covered if I had a monthly payment plan from court, which I do not. I feel like support for those in court and beyond has been neglected and has left me feeling very worried about how to move forwards as my only current income is a small amount of money from my parents and part-time VLE from another movement".

Student Activist

POWER DYNAMICS

The hierarchical and scrutinising structure of the campaign has led to authorative and unbalanced power dynamics that force people into acting as leaders or followers. This adherance to a percieved 'natural hierarchy' of people allows for ageism, puppeteering and mistrust. This creates the conditions for coercive behaviour and consent issues. 

"I left Animal Rebellion when I found out they had gone into coalition with Just Stop Oil, and had countless conversations with people across several groups about my concerns, but kept facing the same responses around the urgency of the crisis and young people's powerlessness to do anything but join existing movements. It broke my heart to see so many young people who had committed so much to the climate movement believing that they couldn't make change by themselves."

Trans Climate Activist

Offering off large sums of money may coerce or unduly influence individuals to take part in high risk actions, comprimising on the voluntariness of consent. Especially if these people are new to activism and come from working class backgrounds. If sleep deprivation and overworking has become the norm due to urgency culture, how do we know Just Stop Oil members are fully ready to consent to high risk actions? Consent is always informed and non-coercive. 

"I would like to start off by saying that I never had high hopes from JSO. My concerns began before JSO publicly launched or took any action. They arose with the knowledge that this was yet another love child of Roger Hallam and his insensitive and bigoted followers.

This preconceived perception was cemented when I was approached and asked to join the movement to take action as a brown, young woman that I know to be unsafe as I have extensive experience in those areas. I was offered a large sum of money and was told “surely you would accept some cash, your homeless, it’s a lot of money for someone like you” and that is true for someone like me who is of no fixed abode. That money, although small to some could have been life changing at the time to me.

If it wasn’t for the fact that I already felt threatened by their seemingly sudden emergence and and my experience in sustained occupations I believe I could have been un-consensually coerced into taking potentially life threatening actions." 

BIPOC Activist

The main issues of your campaign are:

Urgency Culture. This allows the continuation of eco-fascist tactics and narratives under the guise of urgent action. Those who might otherwise stop to challenge oppressive belief systems, that we have all inherited from society, are no longer given the incubation time to do so. Themes of sacrifice, honour, and responsibility​​​​​​​ are leaving people desperate, creating a burnout culture, and setting a dangerous precedent for harm against each other and to ourselves. 

Glorifying Arrest and Prison. Glamourising and celebrating the instruments of violence that the state uses against people of colour and other marginalised groups will always appeal to fascists. Eco-fascism catagorises people into deserving and undeserving, leaving whole groups of people behind in order to suceed e.g. The Maasai being evicted from their homes in the name of conservation.  

Appealing to far right ideologies. Pairing an environmental message within the platforming of racist politicians will appeal to racists and facists. This is making the climate movement vulnerable to infiltration by the far right. When we include people who hold exclusive views into the climate movement, we stop being inclusive.   

Power dynamics. Working from a framework based on a natural hierarchy that some people are just naturally suited to certain things, gives rise to ageism and other isms. This stereotyping is causing distrust, which leads to exclusion of differing views, resulting in people not being able to challenge the power dynamics within the organisation. This promotes coercive behaviour leading to consent issues. This behaviour becomes an opportunity to attract the far right because it is based on domination and essentialism, two of the foundations of fascism. 

Lack of Community Care. The lack of support and aftercare JSO has failed to do, creates a kind of disposability of people. This anti human way of working is reminicent of how the state uses people as pawns and tools for an end goal. Whether intentional or not, acting in ways that neglect our shared humanity creates a growing acceptance to far right ideology.  

Lack of respect for other groups. The idea that 'our way is the only way' is reminicent of colonial thinking. The divide and conquer approach is anti community and anti solidarity. You say you want to build a coalition, but continuing to coopt other groups and imposing your way on them will not work. Real solidarity comes from mutual support behind the scenes (not just for show) and listening to others. 

The colonisation of Asia, Africa and America by the European countries was one of the fascisms that brought this world into a state of disrepair and today the extraction of oil provides the fuel that flows through the veins of very system that we are fighting against. A fascism of today murders, extracts and exploits people for resources in the same way as colonialism. In the west, the same fascism perpetuates that which we are all united against, myths of over-population, greenwashing, and climate apathy primarily leading to the death of the Global South.

Supremacy culture must be relinquished in the face of the biggest crisis in human history because we can not deconstruct this machine with the tools of the oppressor. The fascist ideology of racial, "able" bodied, paternal, neurotypical and cis-heteronormative hierarchy is what caused this problem in the first place.

Whether the reproduction of this culture that is defined in this letter is deliberate or unconscious it must be confronted with care, love, honesty and equality. We must also remember that the disruptive agents of cultural change and the revolutionaries have always been and still are the working class, the serfs, those of marginalised "races", the LGBTQIA+ community, the disabled and the oppressed genders. To systematically exclude us and instead attempt to mobilise the far right will only result in what it has always resulted in, extreme violence, corruption, murder, war and further climate destruction. The visionaries that inspire all activists had one thing in common, that they all created change through anti-normative praxis and not by pandering to the ideologies that created what they were fighting against. 

DEMANDS:
Our demands are simple and will benefit not just you, but the wider climate movement also. You currently have a unique opportunity to set an example for the rest of the movement by reshaping your culture now, before the next phase begins.

1. CHAMPION INFORMED CONSENT

Informed consent creates a community of care and responsibility. Make it clear you could be arrestable whatever you do in Just Stop Oil, whatever the role (this includes social media). As an movement you must take accountability and not abandon those who may have specific needs. 

Let them know the possibility of prison time and let them know prison time is different depending on your identity. This includes BIPOC, working class, neurodivergent, trans and non-binary people. This includes information about treatment at the station, bail conditions and the criminal justice system. The action is not over until the person stops needing support. 

2. PRIORITISE RESOURCES 

Make sure there is adequate funding for wellbeing and community which includes adequate in person support. This could include creativity, retreats or spending time in nature. 

A holistic approach to funding will help you become a more healthy and resilient movement that provides valuable aftercare for those taking action. 

3. EMBED SOLIDARITY

Establish solidarity-based open communication with marginalised communities, where support is given as requested and without co-option of movements. Provide ongoing social justice workshops (including climate refugee solidarity, indigenous communities, victims of fuel poverty, anti racism, deconstructing transphobia) led or supported by those most impacted by the applicable topic. 

We can help you do this. We are inviting you to a training on how we build a truly unified movement. This will include a facilitated discussion space where we can have a dialogue about current issues affecting the climate space. We can provide these online and in person. Please contact us for more information.

To conclude, we would like to inform you that if you choose to not engage with our letter, we will persist in our aims to unify the climate movement and challenge eco fascism wherever we see it; even if it leads to escalation. Those of us from marginalised backgrounds come from generations of resistance and activism and we know how to get things done. 

We are asking you to act and we hope you won't let us down. We are among you and we are within you, so we will be watching closely.

We look forward to hearing from you,

Just Stop Eco Fascism
